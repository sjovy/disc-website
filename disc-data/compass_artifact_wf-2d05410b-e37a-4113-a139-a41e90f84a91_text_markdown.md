DISC traces its origins to William Moulton Marston's 1928 book *Emotions of Normal People*, making it the oldest of the three systems. Because Marston never created an assessment instrument himself, multiple validated versions exist in the public domain.

### DISC scoring algorithm

**Basic scoring method:**
1. Count total selections for each dimension (D, I, S, C)
2. Maximum possible per dimension: 24 points
3. Highest score = primary style; second-highest = secondary style
4. Scores above **65%** of maximum (approximately 16+) indicate high expression

**Profile determination:**
- Identify all dimensions above the midline (65%+)
- Primary style = highest plotting point
- Pattern determined by primary + secondary combination (e.g., "DI" or "SC")

### DISC dimension interpretations

**D = Dominance (Red)**
- **Focus:** Results, challenges, action
- **Motivators:** New challenges, winning, opportunities
- **Fears:** Loss of control, being taken advantage of
- **Behaviors:** Direct, forceful, decisive, competitive, risk-taking
- **Strengths:** Problem solver, self-starter, innovative
- **Limitations:** Oversteps authority, argumentative, insensitive to others

**I = Influence (Yellow)**
- **Focus:** People, enthusiasm, collaboration
- **Motivators:** Social recognition, popularity, approval
- **Fears:** Social rejection, loss of influence
- **Behaviors:** Enthusiastic, trusting, optimistic, expressive, persuasive
- **Strengths:** Great encourager, motivator, creative problem solver
- **Limitations:** Inattentive to detail, disorganized, short attention span

**S = Steadiness (Green)**
- **Focus:** Cooperation, stability, sincerity
- **Motivators:** Harmony, security, sincere appreciation
- **Fears:** Change, loss of security, conflict
- **Behaviors:** Patient, predictable, calm, loyal, supportive
- **Strengths:** Reliable, team player, empathetic, good listener
- **Limitations:** Resistant to change, avoids confrontation, slow to decide

**C = Conscientiousness (Blue)**
- **Focus:** Quality, accuracy, expertise
- **Motivators:** Opportunities for quality work, correctness
- **Fears:** Criticism, being wrong, chaos
- **Behaviors:** Analytical, systematic, cautious, precise, diplomatic
- **Strengths:** Thorough, organized, maintains high standards
- **Limitations:** Bound by procedures, overly critical, analysis paralysis

## Insights Discovery: Jungian framework with 72-position wheel

Insights Discovery was developed by Andi and Andy Lothian in Scotland during the early 1990s, based directly on Carl Jung's 1921 work *Psychological Types*. The system is validated by the British Psychological Society with test-retest reliability of **0.79-0.82** and Cronbach's Alpha of **0.92-0.93**.

### Assessment structure: The Insights Discovery Evaluator

**Format:** 25 frames containing **100 word-pair statements** (4 statements per frame)

**Response method per frame:**
1. Select one word-pair as "Most like me" â†’ **6 points**
2. Select one word-pair as "Least like me" â†’ **0 points**
3. Rate remaining two word-pairs on a **scale of 1-5**

**Sample word-pair items by color:**
- **Cool Blue:** "methodical and logical," "orderly and concise," "exact and precise," "reliable and restrained"
- **Earth Green:** "calm and even-tempered," "diplomatic and calming," "relating and amenable," "accommodating and helping"
- **Sunshine Yellow:** "open and outgoing," "expressive and hopeful," "amiable and quick," "upbeat and hopeful"
- **Fiery Red:** "active and controlling," "determined and resolute," "forceful and goal-oriented," "competitive"

### Scoring methodology

1. Each color receives 25 scores (one per frame), ranging 0-6
2. Calculate **arithmetic mean** across all 25 frames for each color
3. Results produce four color energy scores showing relative preference intensity
4. Wheel position allocated using the **order** of preferences and **differentiation** between scores

### The 72-position wheel structure

The complete wheel contains three concentric rings creating 72 unique positions:

| Ring | Type Name | Description | Population % |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Outer** | Focussed | ONE dominant color/function | ~3% |
| **Middle** | Classic | TWO dominant colors | ~54% |
| **Inner** | Accommodating | THREE colors above midline | ~43% |

**Creative types (24 positions):** Occur when polar opposites (Red/Green or Yellow/Blue) appear as top two colors. These are found in shaded segments and represent individuals with natural tension between competing preferences.

### Conscious vs. Less Conscious personas

Each profile generates **two wheel positions:**
- **Conscious Persona:** How you ACT at work (direct evaluator responses)
- **Less Conscious Persona:** How you REACT under stress (derived responses)

If Conscious position is Focussed, Less Conscious will typically be Accommodating (and vice versa).

### Implementation recommendations

For building assessment tools, DISC and True Colors offer the most accessible starting points due to their public domain or openly published instruments. Insights Discovery's complete Evaluator remains proprietary, though the interpretive framework is well-documented. All three systems should include clear disclaimers that color-based assessments measure preferences, not capabilities, and should not be used for hiring decisions without additional validation for that specific use case.
